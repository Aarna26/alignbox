Over The Hills!

Samantha lives in an isolated village, and has never seen over the mountains. She and her best friend Martha share a dream: To go over the mountains. But what awaits on the other side is not what they dreamed of.

Chapter 1- The Mountains


Over the mountains, I always wonder, what lays there? Ever since I was a little kid, I wondered, "What's on the other side of the mountains?" Mother always said, "Samantha, dear, nothing is over the mountains. They border our world, and over the mountains is nothingness." Of course, I never believed her. I knew someone who has the theory that over the mountains is where you go when you die. Hand in hand you walk with the lord of the afterlife and over the mountains. Someone else I know has the theory that over the mountains there is an ocean full of memories. One sip makes you forget everything, and two sips make you remember anything you want.

My theory is that it is a whole new place, with new people waiting to meet us, or just untamed land full of trees and bushes. Some day, I wish to go over the mountains. Sitting 'round the fire, my village sings. "Over the mountains lies our fears. Over the mountains, pools of tears. Over the mountains streams of gold. Over the mountains, memories grow old. Over the mountains, we wish to see. Over the mountains, count to three. Over the mountains, unknown things. Over the mountains, we make wishing. Climb down the hills of dreams, swim in the shallow stream. And over the mountains, closes our seams..." And so on, so forth. (There's much more to it, it takes 3 hours to sing the full song) Every year on the first of June, my people sing this. It is tradition. And it prepares us for the one day someone will try to venture over the mountains. Nobody has yet. And I will be that person.
